Complaints Procedure

CLC Solicitors is committed to providing a high-quality legal service to all our clients. If something goes wrong, we need you to tell us about it so we can resolve the matter promptly and improve our service.

How to raise a concern

In the first instance, if you have any cause for complaint about any aspect of our service, or with your bill, please contact the person dealing with your matter. They will do their best to resolve your concern informally and without delay.

Formal complaints

If the matter cannot be resolved informally, or if you prefer to make a formal complaint, please forward your written complaint to:

Ms Amrik Baines, Director
CLC Solicitors
21 Lonsdale Road, Queens Park
London NW6 6RA

What happens next

  • We will acknowledge your complaint within 14 days of receiving it.
  • We will provide a full written response within 21 days of acknowledgment, setting out our findings and, where appropriate, any remedial action we propose.
  • If you remain dissatisfied after receiving our response, you may escalate your complaint to the Legal Ombudsman.

Legal Ombudsman

If we are unable to resolve the problem to your satisfaction, you can contact the Legal Ombudsman about your complaint. The Legal Ombudsman deals with complaints about the service you have received from your solicitor. They will look at your complaint independently and it will not affect how we handle your case.

You must refer your complaint to the Legal Ombudsman within six months of receiving a final written response from us. The complaint must relate to an act or omission that occurred no more than six years ago.

Solicitors Regulation Authority

If your complaint relates to the conduct or behaviour of our firm, rather than the quality of service, you may contact the Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) directly:

  • Website: www.sra.org.uk/consumers/problems/report-solicitor

Your bill

You also have the right to object to your bill by applying to the court for an assessment of the bill under Part III of the Solicitors Act 1974. If you exercise this right, the Legal Ombudsman may not consider a complaint about the bill.
